Positioning

Positioning Statement

Guild Grow is an education platform that helps HR and people leaders in midsize-to-large healthcare systems upskill and reskill their workforce. With labor and skill shortages costing healthcare systems millions and compromising patient care, Guild Grow offers turnkey educational programs that attract and retain talent, reduce hiring and turnover costs, and build a resilient workforce ready to meet evolving demands.

The Challenge

Labor and skill shortages are coming.

The U.S. healthcare system faces a workforce crisis of staggering proportions

3.2M
Direct care worker shortfall by 2026
Home health aides, medical assistants, nursing assistants
Source: Mercer
102M
People in Health Professional Shortage Areas
Communities without enough healthcare providers for local populations
Source: Medcerts
500K
Registered nurse shortfall by 2025
U.S. nursing shortages projected to reach critical levels
Source: Mercer
200K–450K
RNs unavailable for direct patient care
Driven largely by burnout and attrition
Source: Oracle
Root Causes

What is driving these shortages?

Burnout, bad management, and limited education capacity will continue to drive these shortages

Burnout

  • Excessive workload and staffing shortages
  • Long and irregular hours
Sources: Adonis, CDC

Management Pressure

  • Micromanagement
  • Focus on metrics over patient care
Source: The Lancet

Limited Educational Capacity

  • Not enough training programs to meet demand
  • Roles stay open longer, turnover remains high, and time-to-hire is rising
Sources: Prolinkworks, trnstaffing
The Toll

These shortages are costing healthcare systems dearly.

The talent crisis is making it harder and more expensive to attract, retain, and develop healthcare workers

Hard to attract talent

Healthcare systems must compete with higher wages, sign-on bonuses, and costly incentives just to fill open roles — while time-to-hire keeps climbing.

83 days Average time to recruit an experienced RN Source: NSI Staffing Report

Hard to retain talent

Remaining staff absorb heavier workloads, accelerating burnout and triggering a vicious cycle of attrition that drains budgets.

$61K Average cost to replace a single staff RN Source: NSI Staffing Report

Hard to develop skills

With limited training capacity and overwhelmed staff, healthcare systems can’t upskill their workforce fast enough to meet evolving care demands.

~50% Share of hospital expenses consumed by labor costs Source: Deloitte
Status Quo

Tuition reimbursement was a start — but it’s not enough.

Employer-sponsored education has been around for decades, yet traditional programs put too much of the legwork on an already distressed workforce

Since 1978, employers have offered up to $5,250/year in tax-free tuition assistance. But most programs require workers to pay upfront and get paid back later — creating barriers for those who need support most.

Financial

Can't afford the upfront costs

Workers cannot afford the upfront cost of tuition, creating an immediate barrier to education and advancement.

2–10% of eligible employees actually use tuition reimbursement Source: Shortlister
Time

No bandwidth to research options

Overworked healthcare workers lack the time to find the right programs.

60% of employees don’t even know their employer offers tuition assistance Source: Bright Horizons
Knowledge

Unclear career direction

Workers don’t know which direction to take to future-proof their careers.

25% of interested employees ever start an application Source: Bright Horizons
